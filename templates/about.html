About this system

GeoFact is a Final Year Project prototype developed at the University of West London, demonstrating the application of Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) to automated fact-checking of geopolitical claims in the USA–China context.

How it works

1

Claim input. You submit a text claim about USA–China relations through the interface.

2

Preprocessing. The claim is cleaned and tokenised using BERT's WordPiece tokeniser with a maximum length of 128 tokens.

3

BERT Classification. A BERT-base-uncased model, fine-tuned on a curated dataset of USA–China geopolitical claims from FEVER and PolitiFact, produces a True/False probability distribution.

4

SHAP Explanation. SHAP (SHapley Additive Explanations) computes token-level feature importance scores, identifying which words most strongly influenced the prediction and in which direction.

5

Result. The verdict, confidence scores, and SHAP token attribution visualisation are displayed together.

Technology stack

Python · PyTorch · HuggingFace Transformers (BERT-base-uncased) · SHAP · Flask · FEVER Dataset · PolitiFact (LIAR Dataset)

Dataset

The model was trained on geopolitical claims filtered from the FEVER dataset (Thorne et al., 2018) and the LIAR dataset (Wang, 2017), which contains PolitiFact-verified claims. Claims were filtered using USA–China domain keywords and mapped to binary True/False labels.

Important limitation: This system is a research prototype. It is not a replacement for professional fact-checking. The model may be incorrect, and SHAP explanations represent learned correlations in training data rather than definitive causal reasoning. Always consult primary sources for important factual claims.
